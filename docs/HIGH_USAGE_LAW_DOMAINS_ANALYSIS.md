## üè† **#5: Tenant-Landlord Laws (Rent Acts)**

### Why It's Important
‚úÖ **Common disputes** (every tenant/landlord faces issues)
‚úÖ **High conflict area** (rent, eviction, deposits)
‚úÖ **Frequent searches** (people actively look for rental rights)
‚úÖ **Both sides need info** (tenants AND landlords)

### Legal Framework
- **State Rent Control Acts** (varies by state)
- **Model Tenancy Act 2021** (central guideline)
- **Transfer of Property Act 1882** (lease provisions)

### Common Issues & User Queries

#### 1. Rent Disputes (Tenant Perspective)
- "Landlord increasing rent unfairly"
- "Forced to pay excessive rent"
- "Not given rent receipt"
- "Rent agreement not made"

**Rights:**
- Rent Control Act limits rent increases
- Right to written rent agreement
- Rent can't be increased arbitrarily
- Receipt mandatory for all payments

#### 2. Eviction Issues (Tenant Perspective)
- "Landlord forcing me to vacate without notice"
- "How much notice period required?"
- "Can I be evicted for not paying one month rent?"
- "Landlord threatening illegal eviction"

**Rights:**
- Cannot be evicted without proper legal notice
- Court order required for eviction
- Valid reasons needed (personal use, default, illegal use)
- Adequate notice period mandatory

#### 3. Security Deposit (Both Sides)
- "Landlord not returning deposit after leaving"
- "Tenant damaged property, can I keep deposit?"
- "How much deposit can be charged?"

**Rights (Tenant):**
- Deposit must be returned within 1-2 months
- Can deduct only for actual damage (with proof)
- Usually 1-3 months rent as deposit limit

**Rights (Landlord):**
- Can deduct for repairs (must prove damage)
- Document property condition at entry/exit
- Bank guarantee as alternative

#### 4. Maintenance & Repairs
- "Property has leakage, landlord not fixing"
- "Who pays for repairs?"
- "Tenant made unauthorized changes"

**Rules:**
- **Landlord:** Structural repairs, major maintenance
- **Tenant:** Daily upkeep, minor repairs
- Emergency repairs: landlord's responsibility

#### 5. Privacy Issues
- "Landlord entering house without permission"
- "Can landlord visit anytime?"

**Rights:**
- Landlord cannot enter without prior notice
- Tenant has right to peaceful enjoyment
- Usually 24-48 hours notice required

### Key Provisions to Cover

1. **Rent Agreements**
   - Registration requirements (>11 months)
   - Essential clauses
   - Stamp duty

2. **Eviction Grounds**
   - Personal requirement
   - Default in rent payment
   - Subletting without permission
   - Illegal use of property

3. **Rent Receipts & Documentation**
   - Mandatory rent receipts
   - Tax implications (HRA)
   - Proof of payment

4. **Dispute Resolution**
   - Rent Controller
   - Civil court procedures
   - Mediation options

### Implementation Effort
**4-5 weeks** - State variations make this complex

### Public Impact
‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê‚≠ê **High** - Millions of tenants + landlords

## üë© **#6: Women Protection Laws (Matrimonial & Violence)**

### Why It's Important
‚úÖ **Half the population** affected
‚úÖ **Serious issues** (domestic violence, dowry, divorce)
‚úÖ **High incidence** (1 in 3 women face violence)
‚úÖ **Empowerment tool** (many women unaware of rights)

### Key Laws Covered

#### A. Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005

**What It Covers:**
- Physical abuse
- Sexual abuse
- Verbal/emotional abuse
- Economic abuse
- Harassment for dowry

**Who Can File:**
- Wife (married/divorced)
- Live-in partner
- Sister, mother, daughter living together
- Any woman in shared household

**Remedies Available:**
- **Protection Order:** Stop abuse immediately
- **Residence Order:** Right to live in matrimonial home
- **Monetary Relief:** Maintenance, medical expenses
- **Custody Order:** Child custody
- **Compensation Order:** For injuries/losses

**How to File:**
- Approach Protection Officer (every district)
- File before Magistrate
- Free legal aid available
- Quick relief (within weeks)

#### B. Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961

**Offences:**
- Giving dowry
- Taking dowry
- Demanding dowry
- Abetting dowry transaction

**Penalties:**
- Minimum 5 years imprisonment
- Minimum ‚Çπ15,000 fine
- Amount of dowry (whichever higher)

**Related BNS Sections:**
- **BNS 84 (IPC 498A):** Cruelty by husband/relatives
- **BNS 80 (IPC 304B):** Dowry death

#### C. Maintenance Rights

**Under CrPC Section 125:**
- Wife can claim maintenance
- Applies even after divorce
- Children can claim till 18 (25 if student)
- Parents can claim from children

**Amount:**
- Court decides based on husband's income
- Usually 25-30% of husband's salary
- Includes children's maintenance

**Under Hindu Marriage Act:**
- Interim maintenance during divorce proceedings
- Permanent alimony after divorce
- One-time settlement option

#### D. Matrimonial Laws (Divorce)

**Grounds for Divorce:**
- Cruelty
- Adultery
- Desertion (1-2 years)
- Conversion to another religion
- Mental disorder
- Mutual consent

**Women's Rights in Divorce:**
- Maintenance/alimony
- Share in property (if acquired jointly)
- Child custody preference (young children)
- Stridhan (woman's own property)

### Common Issues & User Queries

#### 1. Domestic Violence
- "Husband beats me, where to complain?"
- "In-laws harassing for dowry, what to do?"
- "Husband not giving money for household, is it abuse?"
- "Can I stay in house after filing DV case?"

#### 2. Dowry Harassment

- "Threatened to harm if dowry not paid"

#### 3. Divorce & Maintenance

- "Can I get divorce without his consent?"

#### 4. Property Rights
